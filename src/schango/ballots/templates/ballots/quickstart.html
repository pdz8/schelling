Create an account

CrowdVerity uses Ethereum as its backing currency and application platform. Thus every CrowdVerity account requires a funded Ethereum account.

Setup your CrowdVerity account as follows:

  1. Login with Facebook to get to your account profile. Here your name, Ethereum address, Ethereum balance, and registration status are displayed. If it is your first time logging in, we generate a default Ethereum account. (This can be changed.)
  2. New Ethereum accounts need to be registered into CrowdVerity's Ethereum voter pool. Thus you will need to add funds to your account in order to pay Ethereum's gas fees. This can be done by sending Ether to your address or providing the private key of an already funded account. Hit "Register" to finish the process. (Note: CrowdVerity does NOT charge or receive fees.)
  3. Once successfully registered, the text Registered should appear by your Ethereum address. You're then ready to go!

Cast a vote

Voting in CrowdVerity is a two-phase process (see the FAQ for why):

Commit
At the Start Time of a ballot it enters a commit phase. A commit involves paying the deposit for a ballot and committing in secret to an option on the ballot. You can perform a commit simply selecting the vote and clicking "Commit".
Reveal
The Reveal phase begins immediately after the Commit phase. Here you reveal the vote that you previously committed to by selecting it again and clicking "Reveal". This will enable the ballot to be tallied.

Ask a question

Querying the knowledge of CrowdVerity is as simple as going to the ask form, filling it out, and then hitting "Create Contract." We briefly describe the significance of each field here:

Question
This is the question that will be displayed to users. Be clear, concise (256 char limit), and mind the EULA when specifying your query. Additional options can be added/removed their respective buttons.
Deposit
This is the deposit that voters pay when committing to votes and can earn back if truthful. In order to create more voter incentive, this deposit is also charged from the question asker and added to the ballot's pot.
Start Time
The ballot opens for commits at this time. In order to stay within the EULA, make sure the question being asked can be resolved to a single answer at this time.
Commit Period
The length of the commit phase.
Reveal Period
The length of the reveal phase.
